The Science

What Are Exosomes?

Exosomes are small vesicles secreted by stem cells containing large amounts of growth factors, cytokines, and other substances with high regenerative potential. Their unique structure allows them to fuse with other cells and release signaling molecules — facilitating cellular communication and healing.

Unlike stem cells, exosomes do not replicate, do not contain DNA, and do not trigger an immune response — resulting in a safer, cell-free therapeutic product.

Our exosomes are derived from Wharton's Jelly inside donated umbilical cords from full-term, live births — the most robust, plentiful, and ethically appropriate source of regenerative stem cells available.

Why Exosomes Over PRP or Stem Cells?

Live Cells

Stem Cell Therapy

Relatively short lifespan once injected. Rare immune response risks. Much of the therapeutic benefit actually comes from the exosomes stem cells release.
